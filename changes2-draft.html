This file is a draft of the new changes for https://www.jirka.org/ra/changes2.html This list of changes may seem long, but it is essentially just fixing errors and minor issues with the manuscript, mostly found by running it through AI to pinpoint issues. No content was created by AI. A few changes also came from feedback from readers. No numbering changed, there is no new content.
  • Add the condition that \(x^2+y^2=1\) to part a) in Exercise 8.4.6 to fix an erratum. This changes the exercise, but actually makes part b) possible.
  • Add Exercise 8.3.16 with another useful counterexample: directional derivative in all directions at the origin being zero does not imply the function is even continuous.
  • In Exercise 8.6.7 part a), ask for the second derivatives in \(x\) and \(y\) to be 2 rather than 1 to fix an erratum. Without this there would have to be a factor of \(\frac{1}{2}\) in part b).
  • When introducing integration of vector fields in 9.3.2, quickly motivate why we would want to do that (work done).
  • Change Exercise 10.1.6 to fix erratum. In particular, \(h\) must be defined in \(S .\) Avoid the technicality about part of \(R\) being outside \(S\) by adding the assumption that \(R \subset S\) as that is how the exercise is used (one can always replace \(R\) with \(R \cap S\) but let's not make it more complicated.
  • In Exercise 10.1.8, say explicitly the rectangles are closed.
  • The name of the section on the Fubini theorem adds the "the".
  • Example 10.3.8 claim (iii) and Exercise 10.3.10 part b): Change the expression for \(m^*(C_k)\) to the much simpler \(\left(\frac{2}{3}\right)^k .\)
  • Strengthen the hypothesis in Proposition 10.5.8 to assume \(\varphi\) is continuous to fix an erratum. This avoids the upper or lower Darboux integral, and is all we need. This change also changes Exercise 10.5.3 part b) which is to prove this proposition.
  • At the end of Example 10.6.5, when leaving the line integral computation to the reader, emphasize that \(C\) is oriented clockwise and not counterclockwise as is usual.
  • In section 10.7, use \(u\) for the variable on the target to avoid confusion with \(x\) and better connection to calculus. Also write all the equalities in a consistent order which is the typical calc 1 order.
  • Fix issues with the proof of Theorem 10.7.2 (change of variables).
    1. In the change of variables going from \(g'(0)=I\) to a \(g'(0)=A,\) we forgot to keep track of the size of \(A^{-1} ,\) to get the estimate to work out, we need the estimate defining \(W_x\) to be \(< \frac{\epsilon}{2M}\) where \(M\) is the bound on norm of \(g'(x)^{-1} .\)
    2. Give more details in getting the bound for general \(A\) to ensure we get it correct.
    3. The \(x_j\) was supposed to have been picked specifically to make \(|J_g(x)|\) achieve a minimum at \(x_j .\)
    4. The reason why the Darboux style estimate works was glossed over a bit and required the reader to note that \(\delta\) could have been picked smaller to make the partition work as a partition for Darboux. Include the details of this early in the proof and make it more precise.
    5. Remove some of the repeated statements about the reductions to special cases. Also just use \(S\) for the rectangle once we assumed that \(S\) is a rectangle to avoid a new letter.
  • Change Exercise 10.7.3 slightly to make it fit better with the fixed proof of theorem 10.7.2. In particular, change the \(\nicefrac{\epsilon}{2}\) to \(c\) and drop the \(W_x\) name which is not used.
  • In Exercise 10.7.4, be more explicit as to the exact bound we want. Also there is no need to put the prime on the \(\delta.\)
  • In the proof of Proposition 11.4.2, mark the "unimodular" for the index.
  • In Exercise 11.4.11, use \(\arctan\) and \(\arcsin\) for consistency.
  • Make the running title of section 11.5 avoid the "the" to fit on the top header nicely.
  • Replace the outline of Exercise 11.6.11 with the correct outline (using Euler's method) of the proof of Peano's existence theorem.
  • Exercise 11.7.8 changed to fix erratum. Namely, the condition that the function being approximated is equal at the endpoints.
  • Add missing hypothesis to Exercise 11.7.11 that \(X\) has at least two points.
  • In the proof of Parseval (11.8.13) reference Exercise 11.7.8, not just Stone-Weierstrass.
  • Remove instances of "so-called" as it could be misinterpreted.
  • Many other minor improvements in wording and minor clarifications.
